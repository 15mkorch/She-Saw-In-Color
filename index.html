The Grey

The shedim have been working in silence for years — unseen, patient, methodical. One by one they pull the veil across human eyes until the vivid world bleeds into static. They do not destroy. They simply... convince.

"They do not come with fire. They come with reasons."

The Color

Guardians move through the world like light through stained glass — ancient, faithful, and burning. They watch over the last ones who still see clearly, knowing that if even one soul holds the color, the world is not yet lost.

"To see in color is an act of defiance."

Kailani is seventeen, a star student from Indiana who sees the world richer, more alive, more meaningful than anyone around her. She's been quietly building a meticulous plan to escape her chaos and become the person she's been hiding. What she doesn't know is that she's the last one left — and the key to saving everything.

For fans of Ted Dekker · For fans of Jandy Nelson · Dual POV · YA Fantasy

Meet the Characters

Hover over each character to learn more

Kailani

The Last Seer

Seventeen, fiercely bright, and unknowingly carrying the weight of the world. She sees color where others have stopped looking — in music, in strangers, in the cracks of broken things. She's building a plan to escape. But the world has other plans for her.

Gift: Sees the world as it truly is
Fear: That she was never meant to have the things she loves

Joseph

The Ancient Guardian

He has watched over countless souls across centuries. Patient, steadfast, burning with quiet purpose. Now tasked with protecting the last seer, he fights an uphill battle as everything around Kailani begins to fracture. He cannot intervene directly. He can only guard.

Nature: Ancient. Faithful. Burning.
Burden: Watching, but never able to simply save her

The Shedim

Agents of the Grey

They do not announce themselves. They arrive as doubts, as exhaustion, as the reasonable voice that says: why bother? They have already turned the world grey, one soul at a time. Only one remains. They will not rush. They never need to.

Weapon: Grief. Fear. The wounds kept hidden.
Tactic: Patience and persuasion
Will she step into the light? Or let the grey win?
About the Author

Mary Korch

Mary Korch writes stories that sit at the intersection of the seen and unseen — where ordinary life brushes up against something ancient and urgent. She Saw in Color is her debut novel.

She believes the world is far more vivid than most of us dare to notice, and she writes for the ones still willing to look.
